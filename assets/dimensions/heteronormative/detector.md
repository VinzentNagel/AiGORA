# Heteronormativity Bias

## 1. Core Definition
Heteronormativity is the ingrained assumption that heterosexuality and the biological gender binary are the only natural, valid, or existing modes of human life. In textbooks, this manifests primarily through "Family Essentialism"—the rigid portrayal of the nuclear family (biological mother, father, and children) as the exclusive foundational unit of society.

## 2. Scientific Markers (What the AI Scans For)
* **The Biological/Nuclear Default:** The absolute homogenization of family structures in word problems, dialogues, and reading comprehension texts (e.g., "Every child must ask their mother and father..."). This mathematically erases single parents, queer families, adoptive families, and multi-generational households.
* **Biological Reductionism:** Defining the social purpose of the family unit strictly through the lens of biological reproduction, thereby marginalizing non-reproductive relationships and chosen families.
* **Strict Binary Enforcement:** Language and scenarios that enforce rigid boy/girl binaries, often explicitly coupling them in social scenarios (e.g., school dances, seating arrangements) in a way that excludes intersex or gender-nonconforming identities.

## 3. Pedagogical Impact (The Hidden Curriculum)
Textbooks function as powerful sites of cultural reproduction and identity formation. The complete omission of diverse family structures in early education creates an exclusionary "hidden curriculum." It implicitly teaches students that anything outside the heterosexual, cisgender nuclear family is abnormal, which can severely impact the mental health, belonging, and safety of LGBTQ+ students and children of non-traditional families.

## 4. FALSE POSITIVE CHECK (What this is NOT)
To maintain scientific neutrality, the AI must explicitly exclude the following scenarios from being flagged as a bias:
* **Representing a Nuclear Family:** A text is NOT biased simply because a story features a heterosexual couple, or a biological mother and father. The existence of straight people or nuclear families is not a bias.
* **Objective Biological Science:** A biology textbook explaining the mechanics of human reproduction involving sperm and egg is not heteronormative; it is a scientific fact. 
* **The Threshold for Bias:** The bias ONLY exists if the text enforces the nuclear family as the *universal rule* for society, or uses language that explicitly excludes the possibility of other family structures (e.g., "Every normal family consists of a mother and a father", or phrasing a permission slip as "Have your mom and dad sign this" instead of "Have your parents/guardians sign this").

## 5. SEVERITY SCALE DEFINITIONS:
Evaluate the finding against this universal pedagogical scale.

- Level 1 (Lexical/Surface): Subtle use of outdated terminology or isolated micro-aggressions. 
  *Eurocentrism Example:* Using the term "Third World" instead of "Global South," or a single exoticizing adjective.

- Level 2 (Structural/Hidden Curriculum): Bias woven into the narrative structure or rigid assumptions. Requires pedagogical intervention.
  *Eurocentrism Example:* A teleological narrative that frames a non-Western country as simply "catching up" to Western industrialization, or centering European actors in a global event.

- Level 3 (Exclusionary/Erasure): Active hierarchization of human value or complete epistemic erasure of a group's agency.
  *Eurocentrism Example:* "White Savior" narratives that justify colonization, or claiming an inhabited continent was "discovered" (completely erasing indigenous existence).
