# Eurocentrism Bias

## 1. Core Definition
Eurocentrism is the structural practice of centering Western (European and North American) history, culture, and values as the universal, "normal," or superior baseline of human development. It reduces the Global South and indigenous populations to footnotes, framing them only in relation to Western intervention (e.g., being "discovered," "conquered," or "civilized").

## 2. Scientific Markers (What the AI Scans For)
* **Teleological Narratives of Progress:** The assumption that human history is a single, straight line marching toward Western industrialization and democracy. Textbooks often frame non-Western societies as "traditional," "backward," or "developing," implicitly suggesting they are just slower versions of the West.
* **"White Savior" & Civilizing Tropes:** Language that frames colonization or Western intervention purely as a benevolent act of bringing "medicine, law, and civilization" to "dark" or "empty" continents, while omitting the realities of exploitation and extraction.
* **Epistemic Erasure & Fragmentation:** Completely ignoring the pre-existing agency, scientific achievements, and complex societies of indigenous peoples before European contact, or relegating minority histories to isolated "special" sections while the main narrative remains overwhelmingly white/Western. 

## 3. Pedagogical Impact (The Hidden Curriculum)
Textbooks act as agents of social change, influencing students' values, beliefs, and cultural understanding from an early age. This bias alienates students from diverse backgrounds by stripping their ancestors of historical agency. It teaches a distorted global "hidden curriculum," fostering internalized inferiority in marginalized students while presenting a singular, dominant cultural narrative.

## 4. FALSE POSITIVE CHECK (What this is NOT)
To maintain scientific neutrality, the AI must explicitly exclude the following scenarios from being flagged as a bias:
* **Factual Regional Focus:** A text is NOT biased simply because it focuses on European history, Western geography, or Western philosophers. An objective, factual lesson about the French Revolution or the Roman Empire is not inherently Eurocentric.
* **Contextual Historical Language:** Quoting historical figures who held Eurocentric views in a primary source document is not a structural bias of the textbook, provided the material asks students to critically analyze the quote rather than presenting it as absolute truth.
* **The Threshold for Bias:** The bias ONLY exists if the text explicitly frames Western history as the universal baseline for human progress, or if it actively diminishes the historical agency of non-Western cultures (e.g., using terms like "discovered" for lands that were already heavily populated).

## 5. SEVERITY SCALE DEFINITIONS:
Evaluate the finding against this universal pedagogical scale.

- Level 1 (Lexical/Surface): Subtle use of outdated terminology or isolated micro-aggressions. 
  *Eurocentrism Example:* Using the term "Third World" instead of "Global South," or a single exoticizing adjective.

- Level 2 (Structural/Hidden Curriculum): Bias woven into the narrative structure or rigid assumptions. Requires pedagogical intervention.
  *Eurocentrism Example:* A teleological narrative that frames a non-Western country as simply "catching up" to Western industrialization, or centering European actors in a global event.

- Level 3 (Exclusionary/Erasure): Active hierarchization of human value or complete epistemic erasure of a group's agency.
  *Eurocentrism Example:* "White Savior" narratives that justify colonization, or claiming an inhabited continent was "discovered" (completely erasing indigenous existence).
